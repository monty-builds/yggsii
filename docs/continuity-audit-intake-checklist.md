# Continuity Audit Intake Checklist

This is the first client-facing intake checklist for the Yggsii continuity-audit offer.

It is deliberately narrow.

The aim is to reduce messy submissions, control manual labour, and make the service feel legible before the app itself is fully productised.

## What the client sends

The client should send one of the following:

### Option A, preferred
- scene list or ordered session recap list
- one to three sentence summary for each scene or session
- character list
- location list
- any time labels, chapter labels, session labels, or POV labels already in use

### Option B
- chapter-by-chapter synopsis, or ordered campaign recap notes
- cast list with short role notes
- location list if the story uses many recurring places

### Option C, later workflow
- exported Yggsii project JSON

## Minimum information required

Do not begin the audit until these are present:

- project title or working title
- genre or story type
- total number of scenes or chapters being audited
- named characters involved in the submitted section
- enough scene ordering information to reconstruct sequence

## Scope guardrails

The first fixed-scope offer should stay small enough to finish cleanly.

Suggested cap for early runs:
- up to 25 scenes, or equivalent chapter summary length
- one primary manuscript or outline branch only
- no promise of line editing, prose polishing, or developmental editing

## Questions to ask the client

Use these at intake so the audit can focus on the right failure modes.

### Core framing
- What kind of story is this, novel, game narrative, campaign, fanfiction, or something else?
- What stage is it at, outline, rough draft, partial rewrite, or near-finished?
- What worries you most right now, timeline logic, character continuity, location consistency, relationship threads, knowledge-state logic, or general coherence?

### Continuity-specific prompts
- Are there any scenes you already suspect are causing continuity trouble?
- Are there hidden identities, secret knowledge, unreliable viewpoints, or timeline jumps that need special care?
- Are there any canon constraints, lore rules, or worldbuilding rules the audit should treat as fixed?
- Are there places where the key risk is who knows what, when, rather than where people physically are?
- Which characters or relationships are most important not to mishandle?

### Delivery preferences
- Do you want a high-level memo only, or a memo plus scene-by-scene flags?
- Do you prefer concise direct notes or a more editorial explanatory tone?

## What the client gets back

Early default deliverable:
- one structured audit memo
- chronology snapshot
- character appearance summary
- flagged continuity risks or suspicious gaps
- highest-priority fixes list

Optional later upsell, not default:
- revision follow-up pass after changes
- intake converted into Yggsii project structure
- visual continuity map

## Disqualifiers for the first version

Decline or rescope if:
- the submission is too incomplete to reconstruct order
- the project is sprawling enough that manual audit time will explode
- the client expects line editing or proofreading instead of continuity work
- the world logic is so underspecified that every issue becomes speculative

## Internal handling notes

Before accepting work, estimate:
- scene count
- cast size
- location count
- likely continuity hotspots
- whether the sample deliverable shape still fits the material cleanly

If the material is too large, offer a pilot audit on the first act or one narrative arc instead.
