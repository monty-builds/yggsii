# Story Continuity Audit

A fixed-scope narrative debugging pass for messy drafts, outlines, campaigns, and ensemble-heavy projects.

This offer is for writers who do not need line editing yet.

It is for people who need clarity.

## What it helps with

Use this when the story feels broadly right but structurally slippery.

Typical pain points:
- timeline contradictions
- missing character presence logic
- location continuity drift
- dropped relationship threads
- knowledge-state confusion, especially who knows what, when
- secret reveals or canon constraints that stop lining up cleanly
- scenes or session recaps that feel disconnected even though the prose itself is fine

## What the client gets

Default deliverable:
- one structured continuity audit memo
- chronology snapshot
- character appearance summary
- flagged continuity risks, suspicious gaps, or likely breakpoints
- highest-priority fixes list

Optional follow-on later, not default:
- revision follow-up pass
- intake converted into Yggsii project structure
- visual continuity map

## Best fit projects

This is best suited to:
- novels with complicated chronology or ensemble casts
- tabletop campaigns that need recap, adaptation, and continuity control
- game or interactive narrative outlines
- fanfiction with canon constraints, multiple POVs, or large casts
- projects being reshaped from session notes or recap material into cleaner narrative form

## Early scope guardrails

The first version stays intentionally narrow.

Suggested working scope:
- up to 25 scenes, or equivalent chapter-summary length
- one primary manuscript or outline branch only
- continuity and structural coherence only

Not included in the base offer:
- line editing
- copyediting
- proofreading
- full developmental editing
- worldbuilding design from scratch

## Input formats

Preferred input:
- scene list in order, or ordered session recaps
- one to three sentence summary per scene or session
- character list
- location list
- any time labels, chapter labels, session labels, or POV labels already in use

Other workable input:
- chapter-by-chapter synopsis
- cast list with short role notes
- ordered recap notes for campaign or adaptation material
- exported Yggsii project JSON later

## Turnaround shape

Early default promise:
- one fixed-scope audit
- clear written memo
- one revision round on the memo if the client needs clarification

Exact turnaround should depend on scene count and intake quality.

## Positioning

This is not framed as "buy my writing app".

It is framed as:

I will find continuity breaks, missing appearances, chronology tangles, knowledge-state slips, and structural weak points before they turn into rewrite debt.

## Pricing shape, provisional

Do not lock public pricing yet.

Use this internal starting shape instead:
- small pilot audit, first act or one arc
- fixed project audit, capped at the early scope guardrail
- optional follow-up revision pass

Pricing should be tested against effort once one or two real or simulated runs exist.

## Why this offer exists

Selling a rough app is harder than selling a painful outcome reduction.

The continuity audit is a service-shaped wedge into the same problem Yggsii is built to solve.

That means every audit can improve:
- product positioning
- future workflow design
- sample outputs
- trust in the broader narrative debugging idea
